Memento Mori

2018
3-Channel Video and Sound Installation

Expression of sorrow for someone’s death cannot be more blatant than a funeral. But does an assemblage of rituals localised to a single event really suffice for us to conclude our feelings towards the deceased? What happens after and beyond?

Mourning and remembrance is revisited in a more quotient context— lurking in public and transitory spaces. These spaces remain physically consistent. While the emotions and memories of those inhabiting the space alters the associations with these spaces.

The recurring videos are suspended between the stasis and singularity of a still image, disrupted only by flashes of portraiture collages. Enclosed in a stairwell, the accompanying soundscape teases the audience through wavering tunes from a bygone era.
