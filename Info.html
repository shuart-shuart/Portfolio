Hong Shu-ying 方舒颖 (b.1997, Singapore) collects found images and traces of the everyday as part of her research and artistic process. She considers the repetitions and contradictions within these materials by reframing and sequencing them into books, moving images, and other printed matter.

Shu’s works act as reflections and time capsules, exploring alternative knowledge systems and manners of dissemination. She values the everyday and the amateur creations, seeing these as earnest expressions of curiosity, creativity, and dedication. This fascination is deeply rooted in her experiences growing up in amateur Chinese orchestras and consuming user-generated content on the internet. Her recent projects delve into informal archives, such as handwritten elements from scores and annotations, as well as amateur videos and images.

Beyond her artistic practice, Shu produces projects that expand the possibilities of show-making and encounters with art and books, outside of purpose-build spaces. She is also one half of the collective Part Time Book Club alongside Mingli Seet.

Shu was awarded the Kwek Leng Joo Prize of Excellence in Photography and was featured in the 6th Women in Film and Photography Showcase by Objectifs Centre for Photography and Film (Singapore). Most recently, she presented two solo exhibitions at Esplanade (Singapore) during Singapore Art Week 2024 and Yao Alternative Space (Taiwan). Her work has been showcased internationally in exhibitions, festivals, and showcases across Austria, Cambodia, China, Germany, Indonesia, Italy, Malaysia, the Netherlands, South Korea, Taiwan, and Thailand. Shu holds a BFA (Hons) in Media Art (Photography) from Nanyang Technological University's School of Art, Design, and Media.
